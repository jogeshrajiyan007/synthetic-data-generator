## 📊 What This Page Tells You

This page gives you a **comprehensive view of data quality health** across all AXA entities and data fields. It breaks down quality scores by dimension (Completeness, Consistency, Validity) and by data category (Personal, Job, Compensation) — helping you pinpoint exactly where data quality issues are concentrated, down to the individual field level.

---

## 🔢 KPI Definitions

| KPI | What it means |
|-----|--------------|
| **Rows with DQ Errors** | Total number of records that failed at least one data quality rule this week. These are the rows driving all scores below 100%. |
| **Completeness Score** | Percentage of expected fields that are actually populated (non-null). A score below 100% means mandatory fields are missing values. |
| **Consistency Score** | Percentage of records where field values are logically consistent with each other (e.g. contract dates align with employment status). |
| **Validity Score** | Percentage of records where field values conform to defined formats, allowed values, or business rules (e.g. valid country codes, date formats). |
| **Data Quality Score** | The combined overall DQ score across all three dimensions — the single headline number representing data quality for the week. |

## 📈 Visual Breakdown

### DQ Score Details — All Entities (Table)
A field-level breakdown of DQ scores organised by **data category** (Personal, Job) and **individual data point**. Each row shows the Completeness, Consistency, and Validity score for that specific field. Fields showing **"NA"** means that dimension is not applicable or not evaluated for that field. Use this table to:
- Identify which specific fields are dragging down each dimension score
- Distinguish between fields that are partially failing (e.g. EMP_CURR_CONTRACT_TYPE at 78.15% Completeness) vs. fields that are fully unchecked for a dimension

### Data Quality Score by Entity (Bar Chart)
A horizontal bar chart ranking all AXA entities by their overall DQ score for the week. Colour coding reflects score thresholds — green for healthy, yellow/amber for moderate, red for critical. This allows you to immediately see which entities need priority attention (e.g. AXA Ireland at 48.15%, AXA Spain at 55.56%) vs. those performing well (e.g. AXA Morocco at 96.30%, AXA UK at 94.64%).

> 💡 **Hover Interaction — Drill into Entity Details**
> Hovering over any entity's bar reveals a **contextual tooltip panel** with three granular breakdowns:
> - **Rejected Rows Per Dimension** — A donut chart showing how rejected rows are split between Completeness, Consistency, and Validity failures for that entity. For example, hovering over AXA Morocco shows 5 Completeness rejections vs. 36 Validity rejections, immediately telling you the dominant failure type.
> - **DQ Score per Category** — A bar chart breaking the entity's score down by data category: **Compensation**, **Job**, and **Personal**. For AXA Morocco, Job scores 93.74% while Personal scores 98.59%, helping you target remediation at the right data category.
> - This means you don't need to drill to another page to understand an entity's quality profile — a simple hover surfaces the full picture in place.

### Rejected Rows Per Dimension (Donut — on hover)
Shows the proportion of an entity's rejections attributable to each DQ dimension. A large Validity slice means the entity is sending data in wrong formats or with invalid values. A large Completeness slice means fields are simply missing.

> 💡 **Tip:** Cross-reference the **field-level table** with the **entity hover panel** — if an entity has a high Validity rejection count (donut), look for fields in the table with low Validity scores in that same entity's data category.
